About

Hello! My name is Ryan Heidema.

This fall, I will be entering my final semester at the University of Michigan, where I am studying Computer Science Engineering. Scroll down to learn more about who I am and what I do!

Prior to living in Ann Arbor, I had never written a line of code in my life. I came to U of M planning on studying Aerospace Engineering, but my love for coding and computers quickly overtook any aerial/extraterrestrial plans I had.

In my first programming class, ENGR 101, I learned C++ and was instantly intrigued. From here, I enrolled in EECS 280 (Programming & Intro Data Structures), EECS 203 (Discrete Math), and EECS 201 (Computer Science Pragmatics). After enjoying these classes, I officially made the switch and declared my C.S. major in Winter 2020.

Since then, I have enjoyed my time at both UPS and OST doing full-stack web and cross-platform mobile development along with deploying such applications. These experiences, and the great people that went along with them, have taught me a great deal and strengthened my skills as a software engineer.
